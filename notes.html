Written primarily for my own refreshers, shared in case others find them useful. (No client material; all content is general and self-contained.)

Library

Quant Finance Comp Sci

Quant Finance

  • Primer on Itô Calculus PDF
    Motivation-first refresher: why do we need a new calculus for Brownian motion?
  • Primer on NAIC for Hedgers PDF
    Practitioner overview of VM-21 / AG-54 style mechanics and how capital, hedging rules, and scenario frameworks interact for equity-linked insurance books. (Currently a bit “notesy” and lacks flow, but information-dense.)
  • Exploring an SSVI Extension PDF
    A grad-project exploration of an SSVI idea (ultimately not successful - see results), but includes refreshers on smile arbitrage, surface construction, and hedge-consistent pricing (Black–Scholes, vol surfaces, and related foundations).
  • Exploring Alphabetic Bias in Value Stocks PDF
    Another grad-project idea inspired by my own experience using stock screeners.
  • Option Implied Asset Allocation Using Levy Processes PDF
    My Master's thesis. There are numerous methodological issues I could not recognize at the time (blame no practicing experience at the time..), however this has good refreshers on topics like Levy processes and Fourier pricing.
  • SOFR-based Risk Free Curve Bootstrapping PDF
    Refresher on risk-free curve bootstrapping. A good review of how simple the process has become now that the dual-curve complexity has passed with the LIBOR transition over and SOFR swaps being liquid for many terms.

Comp Sci

  • Applied Data Structures in C# PDF
    Review of what actually happens under the hood for various popular (at least in my experience) data structures and containers.
  • Object Oriented Design Principles and Practice PDF
    A huge review of a three-part grad class I took on OOAD. Nothing original here and this rips so much off from GOF, but I wanted to get everything in one place to create a useful master reference.
  • Network Systems Foundations PDF
    Another review inspired by a grad class. A great primer to answer the question "how does my computer actually even talk to the outside world?". Also see the short companion notes on cyclic redundancy checks below.
  • Cyclic Redundancy Checks PDF
    A small companion note to the Network Systems Foundations primer.
